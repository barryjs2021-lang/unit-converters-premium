Molar flow rate measures the amount of substance (in moles) passing through a system per unit time. This is fundamental in chemical engineering, reaction kinetics, and process design.

Common molar flow units:
‚Ä¢ **mol/s (mole per second)**: SI unit for molar flow rate. Standard for chemical engineering calculations.
‚Ä¢ **mol/min, mol/h**: Common time-based variations for different process scales.
‚Ä¢ **kmol/s, kmol/h**: Used for large-scale industrial processes and plant design.
‚Ä¢ **mmol/s, Œºmol/s**: Used for analytical chemistry, laboratory work, and micro-scale processes.
‚Ä¢ **lb-mol/h**: Imperial unit used in US chemical industry and older plant designs.

Applications by scale:
- **Industrial (kmol/h)**: Large chemical plants, refineries, ammonia synthesis
- **Pilot Plant (mol/min)**: Process development, reaction optimization
- **Laboratory (mmol/min)**: Synthesis reactions, analytical procedures
- **Analytical (Œºmol/s)**: Chromatography, spectrometry, trace analysis

Molar flow rate is important in:
- **Chemical Reaction Engineering**: Reactor design, conversion calculations
- **Process Design**: Material balances, separation unit sizing
- **Catalysis**: Catalyst activity, space velocity calculations
- **Environmental Engineering**: Emission rates, treatment efficiency
- **Biochemical Engineering**: Fermentation, enzyme kinetics

- Calculating reaction stoichiometry and conversion rates
- Designing chemical reactors and separation equipment
- Determining catalyst requirements and space velocities
- Optimizing process yields and selectivity
- Scaling laboratory reactions to industrial processes
