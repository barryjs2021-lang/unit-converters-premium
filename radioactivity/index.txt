Radioactivity is the spontaneous decay of unstable atomic nuclei, releasing ionizing radiation. It's measured as the number of disintegrations per unit time.

**SI Unit:**
• **Becquerel (Bq)** - 1 disintegration per second

**Legacy Unit:**
• **Curie (Ci)** - 3.7×10¹⁰ disintegrations per second
• Originally based on activity of 1 gram of radium-226

**Key characteristics:**
• **Random process** - follows exponential decay law
• **Half-life** - time for activity to reduce by half
• **Specific activity** - activity per unit mass (Bq/g)
• **Activity concentration** - activity per unit volume (Bq/L)

**Nuclear medicine applications:**
• **Diagnostic imaging** - typically MBq levels
• **Therapeutic applications** - GBq to TBq levels
• **Radiopharmaceuticals** - specific activity formulations
• **Quality control** - calibration standards

**Common radioisotopes:**
• **Tc-99m** - Most common diagnostic isotope (6-hour half-life)
• **F-18** - PET imaging (110-minute half-life)
• **I-131** - Thyroid therapy (8-day half-life)
• **Lu-177** - Neuroendocrine tumor therapy (6.7-day half-life)
• **Ra-223** - Bone metastases therapy (11.4-day half-life)

**Measurement techniques:**
• **Dose calibrators** - ionization chambers for activity measurement
• **Gamma counters** - well-type detectors
• **Liquid scintillation** - for beta emitters
• **Geiger-Müller detectors** - contamination monitoring

**Regulatory considerations:**
• **Licensed activities** - require regulatory approval
• **Transport regulations** - specific packaging requirements
• **Waste disposal** - decay storage and disposal protocols
• **Personnel monitoring** - radiation safety programs

**Activity calculations:**
- **Decay equation**: A(t) = A₀ × e^(-λt)
- **Half-life relation**: t₁/₂ = ln(2)/λ
- **Specific activity**: Activity per unit mass or volume

Understanding radioactivity units is crucial for nuclear medicine technologists, medical physicists, radiation safety officers, and healthcare professionals working with radioactive materials.
