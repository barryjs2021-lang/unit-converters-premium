Luminous flux is the total quantity of visible light emitted by a source. It represents the total amount of light produced, regardless of direction, and is fundamental for lighting calculations and LED specifications.

The SI unit for luminous flux is the lumen (lm), which is derived from the candela (the base SI unit for luminous intensity).

**Key characteristics:**
• **Total light output** - measures all light emitted in all directions
• **Wavelength weighted** - based on human eye sensitivity (photopic vision)
• **Conservation** - preserved in lossless optical systems
• **Independent of direction** - total amount regardless of beam pattern

**Common light source outputs:**
• **Candle**: 12.6 lm
• **Incandescent 40W**: 450 lm
• **Incandescent 60W**: 800 lm
• **Incandescent 100W**: 1600 lm
• **CFL 13W**: 800 lm
• **LED 10W**: 800-1000 lm
• **Fluorescent T8**: 2800-3200 lm
• **HID 250W**: 20,000-25,000 lm

**LED technology:**
• **High-efficiency LEDs**: 100-200 lm/W
• **Standard LEDs**: 80-120 lm/W
• **Older LEDs**: 40-80 lm/W
• **Theoretical maximum**: ~680 lm/W (white light)

**Applications:**
• **LED specification** - manufacturer ratings
• **Lighting design** - total light budget calculations
• **Energy efficiency** - lumens per watt (lm/W)
• **Photometry** - light measurement and testing
• **Architectural lighting** - space illumination planning
• **Horticulture** - grow light specifications

**Measurement standards:**
• **Integrating sphere** - measures total flux output
• **Goniophotometry** - directional light distribution
• **CIE standards** - international photometric practices
• **LM-79** - LED testing procedures (North America)
• **LM-80** - LED lumen maintenance testing

**Key relationships:**
- **Luminous flux = Luminous intensity × Solid angle**
- **Illuminance = Luminous flux / Area**
- **Luminous efficacy = Luminous flux / Electrical power (lm/W)**
- **For isotropic source**: Φ = 4π × I (lumens = 4π × candela)

**Design considerations:**
• **Beam angle** - affects illuminance distribution
• **Lumen maintenance** - light output over time
• **Color temperature** - affects perceived brightness
• **Driver efficiency** - overall system efficacy

Understanding luminous flux is essential for lighting designers, LED engineers, facility managers, and anyone specifying or comparing light sources for their total light output.
