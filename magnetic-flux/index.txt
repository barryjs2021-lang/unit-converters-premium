Magnetic flux is the measure of the total magnetic field passing through a given area. It represents the number of magnetic field lines penetrating a surface and is fundamental to electromagnetic induction.

The SI unit for magnetic flux is the Weber (Wb), which is equivalent to volt-second (V·s).

**Key characteristics:**
• **Scalar quantity** - total field through surface (dot product B·A)
• **Faraday's law** - changing flux induces voltage
• **Conservation** - total flux entering equals flux leaving (no magnetic monopoles)
• **Area dependent** - flux = magnetic field × area × cos(angle)

**Fundamental relationships:**
• **Φ = B·A** (magnetic flux = field × area)
• **EMF = -dΦ/dt** (Faraday's law of induction)
• **Φ = L·I** (flux linkage = inductance × current)

**Applications:**
• **Electromagnetic induction** - transformers and generators
• **Electric motors** - torque generation and control
• **Magnetic coupling** - wireless power transfer
• **Flux linkage** - inductor and transformer design
• **Magnetic circuits** - reluctance and permeance calculations
• **Superconducting devices** - flux quantization in loops

**Measurement techniques:**
• **Search coils** - voltage measurement from flux change
• **Hall effect sensors** - integrated flux measurements
• **Fluxmeters** - direct flux measurement instruments
• **Ballistic galvanometers** - flux change detection
• **Digital fluxmeters** - modern electronic instruments

**Design considerations:**
• **Core materials** - ferromagnetic vs. air core
• **Saturation effects** - maximum flux density limits
• **Hysteresis losses** - energy dissipation in AC applications
• **Leakage flux** - field lines not linking all windings
• **Fringing effects** - field spreading at air gaps

**Electromagnetic devices:**
• **Transformers**: 0.001-10 Wb (depending on size and voltage)
• **Electric motors**: 0.01-1 Wb (varies with power rating)
• **Generators**: 0.1-100 Wb (large power plants)
• **Inductors**: 1 μWb - 1 mWb (electronic circuits)
• **MRI magnets**: 0.01-1 Wb (imaging coils)

**Flux quantization:**
In superconducting loops, magnetic flux is quantized in units of:
• **Flux quantum**: Φ₀ = h/2e = 2.07×10⁻¹⁵ Wb
• **SQUID devices** - utilize flux quantization for sensitivity
• **Josephson junctions** - quantum flux effects

**Key relationships:**
- **Weber's definition**: 1 Wb = 1 V·s = 1 kg·m²/(A·s²)
- **Maxwell (CGS)**: 1 Wb = 10⁸ Mx
- **Inductance**: L = Φ/I (henries = webers/ampere)

Understanding magnetic flux is essential for electrical engineers, motor designers, transformer specialists, and anyone working with electromagnetic induction, magnetic circuits, or superconducting devices.
