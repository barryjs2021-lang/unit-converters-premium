About This Converter

Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity with respect to time. It indicates how quickly an object speeds up, slows down, or changes direction. Common acceleration units: • **m/s² (meter per second squared)**: The SI unit of acceleration. Represents the change in velocity (m/s) per second. • **ft/s² (foot per second squared)**: Imperial unit commonly used in engineering applications. • **g (standard gravity)**: Equal to Earth's gravitational acceleration (≈9.80665 m/s²). Used for expressing high accelerations. • **Gal (galileo)**: Equal to 1 cm/s². Used in geophysics and seismology for measuring small accelerations. • **in/s² (inch per second squared)**: Used in some engineering applications. Key relationships: - 1 g = 9.80665 m/s² = 32.174 ft/s² - 1 Gal = 0.01 m/s² = 0.001019 g - Acceleration due to gravity varies slightly by location on Earth Acceleration is important in: - **Automotive**: Vehicle performance, braking distances - **Aerospace**: G-forces on pilots and passengers - **Civil Engineering**: Seismic design, earthquake analysis - **Physics**: Motion analysis, force calculations - **Safety**: Impact analysis, protective equipment design - **Sports**: Athletic performance analysis Understanding acceleration helps in: - Vehicle safety design and testing - Structural engineering for earthquake resistance - Space mission planning and astronaut training - Industrial equipment design and operation
