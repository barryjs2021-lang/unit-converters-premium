About This Converter

Astronomical distances span an enormous range, from planetary radii to cosmological scales. These units are essential for describing the vast scales of the universe, from our solar system to distant galaxies and the observable universe. **Key astronomical distance units:** **Solar System Scale:** • **Astronomical Unit (AU)** - Earth-Sun distance = 149.6 million km • **Light Second/Minute/Hour/Day** - Distance light travels in given time • **Solar Radius (R☉)** - Radius of the Sun = 696,000 km • **Earth Radius (R⊕)** - Radius of Earth = 6,371 km • **Lunar Distance** - Earth-Moon distance = 384,400 km **Stellar Scale:** • **Light Year (ly)** - Distance light travels in one year = 9.46×10¹² km • **Parsec (pc)** - Distance at which 1 AU subtends 1 arcsecond = 3.26 ly • **Kiloparsec (kpc)** - 1,000 parsecs, galactic scale distances • **Megaparsec (Mpc)** - 1 million parsecs, intergalactic distances **Cosmological Scale:** • **Gigaparsec (Gpc)** - 1 billion parsecs, large-scale structure • **Hubble Distance** - c/H₀ ≈ 14.4 Gpc (observable universe scale) **Distance measurement techniques:** • **Radar ranging** - Planets and asteroids (up to ~50 AU) • **Parallax** - Nearby stars (up to ~500 pc with Gaia) • **Standard candles** - Cepheids, Type Ia supernovae (Mpc scale) • **Surface brightness fluctuations** - Galaxy distances • **Redshift** - Cosmological distances (Hubble's law) **Key distance benchmarks:** • **Moon**: 1.28 light seconds = 384,400 km • **Sun**: 8.32 light minutes = 1 AU • **Nearest star**: Proxima Centauri = 4.24 ly = 1.30 pc • **Galactic center**: ~8.5 kpc = 27,700 ly • **Andromeda Galaxy**: 2.54 Mpc = 8.28 million ly • **Virgo Cluster**: ~16.5 Mpc = 54 million ly • **Observable universe**: ~14.4 Gpc = 47 billion ly **Cosmological applications:** • **Hubble's law**: v = H₀d (recession velocity proportional to distance) • **Distance ladder**: Building up from local to cosmological scales • **Parallax measurements**: Foundation of astronomical distance scale • **Type Ia supernovae**: Standard candles for cosmological distances • **Cosmic microwave background**: Last scattering surface at z≈1100 **Scale relationships:** • **1 parsec = 3.26 light years = 206,265 AU** • **1 light year = 63,241 AU = 9.46×10¹² km** • **1 AU = 499 light seconds = 1.496×10⁸ km** • **1 Solar radius = 109 Earth radii = 696,000 km** **Observational constraints:** • **Parallax limit**: Ground-based ~100 pc, Gaia satellite ~2 kpc • **Cepheid variables**: Reliable to ~30 Mpc with HST • **Type Ia supernovae**: Observable to z>1 (>5 Gpc) • **Surface brightness fluctuations**: Galaxy distances to ~100 Mpc **Modern precision:** • **Gaia mission**: Microarcsecond parallax precision • **JWST observations**: Pushing distance measurements to z>10 • **Gravitational wave detectors**: Independent distance measurements • **21cm hydrogen line**: Potential dark age observations Understanding astronomical distances is crucial for astrophysics, cosmology, stellar astronomy, galactic astronomy, and space mission planning across all scales of the universe.
