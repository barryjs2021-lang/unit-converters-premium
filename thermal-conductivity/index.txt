Specific heat (also called specific heat capacity) is the amount of energy required to raise the temperature of one unit mass of a substance by one degree of temperature. It is an intensive property that depends on the material but not on the amount of material.

The SI unit for specific heat is joules per kilogram kelvin (W/(mÂ·K)). In the Imperial system, BTU per pound Fahrenheit (BTU/(lbÂ·Â°F)) is commonly used.

- Food processing and cooking
- Chemical process design
- Climate modeling
