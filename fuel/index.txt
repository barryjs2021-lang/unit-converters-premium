Fuel consumption measures how efficiently a vehicle uses fuel, expressed in different units depending on the region and measurement system.

Common fuel consumption units:
• **L/100km (Liters per 100 kilometers)**: Used in most metric countries. Lower numbers indicate better efficiency.
• **MPG (Miles per Gallon)**: Used primarily in the US and UK. Higher numbers indicate better efficiency.
• **km/L (Kilometers per Liter)**: Used in some countries. Higher numbers indicate better efficiency.
• **Gallons per 100 miles**: Less common, used for heavy vehicles. Lower numbers indicate better efficiency.

Key points:
- L/100km and mpg are inversely related (lower L/100km = higher mpg)
- These measurements help compare vehicle efficiency
- Actual consumption varies with driving conditions, vehicle condition, and driving style

Fuel efficiency is important for:
- **Environmental Impact**: Lower consumption reduces emissions
- **Cost Management**: Better efficiency saves money on fuel
- **Vehicle Selection**: Comparing different cars and engines
- **Trip Planning**: Calculating fuel needs for long journeys
- **Fleet Management**: Optimizing business vehicle costs

Understanding fuel consumption units helps make informed decisions about vehicle purchases, trip costs, and environmental impact.
