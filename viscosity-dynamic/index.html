About This Converter

Dynamic viscosity (also called absolute viscosity) is a measure of a fluid's resistance to shear deformation. It quantifies the internal friction within a fluid when layers of the fluid move relative to each other. The SI unit for dynamic viscosity is pascal second (Pa·s). However, in practice, centipoise (cP) is widely used because it provides convenient numerical values for most common fluids. **Key relationships:** • **1 Pa·s = 1000 cP = 10 P** • **Water at 20°C ≈ 1 cP** (convenient reference) • **Higher viscosity** = thicker fluid, more resistance to flow • **Lower viscosity** = thinner fluid, flows more easily **Common dynamic viscosity values:** • **Air (20°C)**: 0.018 cP - very low viscosity gas • **Water (20°C)**: 1.002 cP - reference standard • **Motor oil SAE 30**: 300 cP - typical lubricating oil • **Honey**: 10,000 cP - very viscous liquid • **Bitumen**: 100,000,000 cP - extremely viscous **Applications:** - Fluid mechanics and pipe flow calculations - Pump and turbine design - Lubrication engineering - Chemical process design - Food and pharmaceutical manufacturing - Paint and coating formulation Understanding dynamic viscosity is essential for designing fluid systems, selecting appropriate pumps, calculating pressure drops, and ensuring proper fluid handling in industrial processes.
