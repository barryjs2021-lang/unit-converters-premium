Fuel efficiency by mass measures how far a vehicle can travel per unit mass of fuel consumed. This metric is particularly useful for comparing different fuel types and understanding the energy density impact on transportation efficiency.

Common fuel efficiency-mass units:
• **km/kg (kilometer per kilogram)**: SI unit for fuel efficiency by mass. Used in scientific and engineering calculations.
• **mi/lb (miles per pound)**: Imperial unit commonly used in aviation and marine applications.
• **km/tonne (kilometers per metric ton)**: Used for heavy vehicles, ships, and industrial applications.
• **m/g (meters per gram)**: Used for precise measurements and small-scale applications.
• **ft/oz (feet per ounce)**: Used for small engines and model vehicles.

Key relationships:
- Higher values indicate better fuel efficiency
- 1 km/kg = 0.4536 mi/lb = 1000 m/g
- Different from volume-based efficiency (mpg, km/L)
- Relates directly to fuel energy density

Fuel efficiency by mass is important in:
- **Aviation**: Aircraft performance analysis, fuel planning
- **Marine Transport**: Ship efficiency, cargo optimization
- **Heavy Industry**: Mining equipment, construction machinery
- **Alternative Fuels**: Comparing hydrogen, electric, biofuel efficiency
- **Research**: Energy density studies, fuel technology development

Understanding mass-based efficiency helps in:
- Comparing different fuel types on energy content basis
- Optimizing payload vs fuel consumption trade-offs
- Evaluating alternative propulsion technologies
- Calculating total system efficiency including fuel weight
