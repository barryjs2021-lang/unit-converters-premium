Magnetomotive force (MMF) is the magnetic equivalent of electromotive force (EMF). It represents the driving force that produces magnetic flux in a magnetic circuit, analogous to voltage in an electric circuit.

The SI unit for magnetomotive force is the ampere-turn (At), which represents the magnetic force produced by one ampere of current flowing through one turn of a coil.
