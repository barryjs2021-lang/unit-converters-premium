About This Converter

Permeability measures a material's ability to allow fluids to pass through it. It's fundamental in petroleum engineering, hydrology, membrane science, and porous media studies. Common permeability units: • **m² (square meter)**: SI unit for intrinsic permeability, represents actual area available for flow. • **D (darcy)**: Industry standard unit in petroleum engineering, named after Henry Darcy. • **mD (millidarcy)**: Most common unit for reservoir rocks and practical petroleum applications. • **μD, nD**: Used for tight formations, shales, and ultra-low permeability materials. • **barrer**: Gas permeability unit used for membranes and barrier materials. Key relationships: - Darcy's Law: Q = (kA/μ) × (ΔP/L) - 1 D = 9.869233 × 10⁻¹³ m² ≈ 10⁻¹² m² - 1 mD = 10⁻³ D = 9.87 × 10⁻¹⁶ m² - 1 barrer = 3.347 × 10⁻¹⁶ m² ≈ 3.4 × 10⁻⁴ D Permeability ranges by material: - **Very High (>1 D)**: Gravel, coarse sand, fractured rock - **High (100-1000 mD)**: Good reservoir sandstone, limestone - **Medium (10-100 mD)**: Average reservoir quality rock - **Low (1-10 mD)**: Tight formations, fine-grained rocks - **Very Low (<1 mD)**: Shales, tight gas formations - **Ultra-Low (nD range)**: Source rocks, sealing formations Factors affecting permeability: - **Porosity**: Higher porosity generally increases permeability - **Pore Size**: Larger pores allow easier fluid flow - **Connectivity**: Well-connected pore network essential for flow - **Fluid Properties**: Viscosity affects apparent permeability - **Pressure**: Stress can reduce permeability in soft rocks Permeability is important in: - **Petroleum Engineering**: Reservoir characterization, production forecasting - **Hydrology**: Groundwater flow, aquifer assessment, contamination studies - **Civil Engineering**: Dam design, foundation analysis, soil drainage - **Chemical Engineering**: Membrane separation, catalyst design - **Environmental Engineering**: Landfill liners, soil remediation Understanding permeability helps in: - Predicting fluid flow rates through porous media - Designing efficient oil and gas recovery strategies - Selecting appropriate membranes for separation processes - Assessing environmental contamination and remediation potential
