Mass flow rate is the mass of fluid passing through a given cross-sectional area per unit time. It is a fundamental parameter in fluid mechanics, chemical engineering, and process design.

The SI unit for mass flow rate is kilogram per second (kg/s). In industrial applications, depending on the scale and region, various other units are commonly used such as ton per hour (t/h) or pound per hour (lb/h).

**Key characteristics:**
‚Ä¢ **Independent of fluid density changes** (unlike volumetric flow rate)
‚Ä¢ **Conservation principle**: mass in = mass out (steady state)
‚Ä¢ **Related to volumetric flow by density**: ·πÅ = œÅ √ó Q
‚Ä¢ **Critical for material balance calculations**

**Common mass flow rate ranges:**
‚Ä¢ **Household faucet**: 0.1-0.5 kg/s
‚Ä¢ **Automotive fuel injection**: 0.001-0.01 kg/s
‚Ä¢ **Industrial steam lines**: 1-100 kg/s
‚Ä¢ **Power plant cooling water**: 1000-10,000 kg/s
‚Ä¢ **Chemical processing**: 0.01-1000 kg/s

**Applications:**
- Chemical process design and control
- Power plant steam and cooling systems
- Fuel systems in automotive and aerospace
- HVAC air handling calculations
- Food and pharmaceutical manufacturing
- Environmental monitoring and emissions

**Design considerations:**
- Pipe sizing and pressure drop calculations
- Pump and compressor selection
- Heat exchanger design
- Material balance and yield calculations
- Process control and instrumentation

Understanding mass flow rate is essential for process engineering, system design, and ensuring proper material handling in industrial applications.
