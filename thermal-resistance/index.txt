Thermal resistance is a measure of how much a material or assembly resists the flow of heat. It is the reciprocal of thermal conductance and represents the temperature difference required to sustain a unit of heat flow rate.

The SI unit for thermal resistance is kelvin per watt (K/W). In HVAC and building applications, thermal resistance is often expressed in Imperial units such as °F·h/BTU.

**Key concepts:**
• **Lower thermal resistance** = better heat transfer (good for heat sinks)
• **Higher thermal resistance** = better insulation (good for building walls)
• **Total resistance** = sum of individual resistances in series

**Applications:**
- Electronic thermal management (heat sinks, thermal pads)
- Building insulation design (R-values)
- Heat exchanger design
- Thermal barrier coatings
- HVAC system analysis

**Common thermal resistance values:**
• **CPU heat sinks**: 0.1-2 K/W
• **Thermal interface materials**: 0.01-0.5 K/W
• **Building insulation**: 1-10 K/W
• **Air gaps**: 0.1-1 K/W

Understanding thermal resistance is essential for thermal design, energy efficiency calculations, and temperature control in various engineering applications.
