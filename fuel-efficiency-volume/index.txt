Fuel efficiency by volume measures how far a vehicle can travel per unit volume of fuel consumed. This is the most common way to express fuel economy for road vehicles and is directly related to operating costs.

Common fuel efficiency-volume units:
• **km/L (kilometer per liter)**: Metric standard used globally, intuitive for most countries.
• **mpg (miles per gallon)**: Imperial unit used in US and UK, with different gallon sizes.
• **L/100km (liters per 100 kilometers)**: Consumption-based metric used in Europe, lower is better.
• **m/mL (meters per milliliter)**: Precise measurements for small engines and research.
• **gal/100mi (gallons per 100 miles)**: US consumption-based metric, lower is better.

Key relationships:
- Higher km/L and mpg values indicate better efficiency
- Lower L/100km and gal/100mi values indicate better efficiency
- 1 km/L = 2.352 mpg (US) = 1.957 mpg (UK)
- L/100km = 100 ÷ km/L (inverse relationship)

Volume-based efficiency factors:
- **Engine Technology**: Hybrid, turbo, direct injection effects
- **Vehicle Weight**: Affects fuel consumption significantly
- **Driving Conditions**: City vs highway efficiency differences
- **Fuel Quality**: Octane rating and additives impact

Fuel efficiency by volume is important in:
- **Automotive Industry**: Vehicle ratings, consumer comparisons
- **Fleet Management**: Operating cost calculations, route optimization
- **Environmental Policy**: Emissions standards, fuel economy regulations
- **Consumer Economics**: Fuel cost budgeting, vehicle purchasing decisions

Understanding volume-based efficiency helps in:
- Comparing vehicle fuel economy across different markets
- Calculating fuel costs for trips and daily driving
- Evaluating the economic impact of fuel-efficient technologies
- Making informed decisions about vehicle purchases and usage
